caLAB Sample Overview
A sample is created and assigned an ID when it arrives at the laboratory. A sample coming into a nanotech laboratory is a nanoparticle. Multiple lots for the same sample can be received. Because of that, a lot id is appended to the sample ID. NCL-22-1 (sample ID “22” + lot ID “1”). When performing an experiment, multiple aliquots for each sample/lot are created and experimental data is submitted for these aliquots in the workflow. When the experiment is complete and the researcher wants to publish the outcome of the experiment, the final results files associated with the sample + lot are submitted/selected into the system. The final results apply to all aliquots associated with the sample and lot. Annotations are recorded at the sample (nanoparticle) level.
Note:
In caLAB, the term “sample” can refer to either of two entities: 1) A Sample is identified by a vendor, a sample arrival date; this is the sample identified when creating an Aliquot; and 2) a Nanoparticle sample is annotated with research and other characterization data. In this online help, the first sample is identified as Sample and the second sample is identified as Nanoparticle.
See also Creating a New Sample and Annotating a Nanoparticle.
