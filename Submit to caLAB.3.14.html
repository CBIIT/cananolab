Complex Particle
A complex particle is defined as a nanoparticle bound to another moiety of interest via a chemical or physical interaction.
Each Composition form starts with a summary section that names the particle source. To complete the form, follow these steps:
1.
Fill in each of the following fields on the Complex Particle characterization form:
Complex Particle Characterization Fields
Definition
Characterization Source
Enter the source from which the characterization data is derived, such as a vendor or a laboratory (NCL).
View Title*
Enter a unique and meaningful name for the characterization. The title you enter will display on the Particle Tree. The asterisk indicates a required field.
Description
Enter any additional characterization information that the form does not include.
Composing Nanoparticle Information
 
Number of Composing Nanoparticles
Enter the number of nanoparticles comprising the complex particle.
Update Composing Nanoparticles
Note: If you enter any number greater than “0”, click the Update Composing Nanoparticles button at the bottom of the page. This extends the page with the corresponding number of additional fields so you can enter additional information for each unit.
