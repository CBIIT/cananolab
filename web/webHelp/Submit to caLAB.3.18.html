Polymer
A key feature that distinguishes polymers from other molecules is the repetition of a linked series of many identical, similar, or complementary monomers. A polymer nanoparticle is just that--a polymer measured in nanometer range.
Each Composition form starts with a summary section that names the particle source. To complete the form, follow these steps:
1.
Fill in each of the following fields on the Polymer characterization form:
Polymer Characterization Fields
Definition
Characterization Source
Enter the source from which the characterization data is derived, such as a vendor or a laboratory (NCL).
View Title*
Enter a unique and meaningful name for the characterization. The title you enter will display on the Particle Tree. The asterisk indicates a required field.
Description
Enter any additional characterization information that the form does not include.
Composition Properties
 
Is crosslinked
Crosslinking is a covalent bond between two polymers or two different regions of the same polymer. Select Yes or No, indicating whether the polymer is crosslinked.
Crosslink Degree
Enter the percentage level of covalent linkage in the polymer.
Initiator
Select from the drop down list, the agent that initiated the polymerization.
