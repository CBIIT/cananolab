Emulsion
An emulsion is a suspension of liquid within another liquid or a dispersion consisting of two or more liquid phases. In the context of caNanoLab, an emulsion nanoparticle consists of nanoparticles suspended in an emulsifying liquid.
Each Composition form starts with a summary section that names the particle source. To complete the form, follow these steps:
1.
Fill in each of the following fields on the Emulsion characterization form:
Emulsion Characterization Fields
Definition
Characterization Source
Enter the source from which the characterization data is derived, such as a vendor or a laboratory (NCL).
View Title*
Enter a unique and meaningful name for the characterization. The title you enter will display on the Particle Tree. The asterisk indicates a required field.
Description
Enter any additional characterization information that the form does not include.
Composition Properties
 
Emulsion Type
Enter the emulsion type to which the emulsion belongs.
Molecular formula
Enter the chemical formula for the emulsion.
Is Polymerized
Polymerization consists of enzymatic reactions that link a series of monomers, forming a polymerized compound (polymer), usually of high molecular weight, by combination of simpler molecules (monomers).
Select Yes or No, reflecting whether or not the emulsion is polymerized.
Polymer Name
Enter the name of the polymer suspended in the emulsion.
2.
When you have finished, click Submit or click Reset to clear all fields on the form.
