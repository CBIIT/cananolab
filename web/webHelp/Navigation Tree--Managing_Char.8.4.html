Characterization Details View
In caNanoLab, Nanoparticle Characterization is defined with Physical and In Vitro Characterizations. Molecular Weight is an example of a Physical Characterization category. Oxidative Stress is an example of an In Vitro Characterization.
Characterizations that have been added are listed under their corresponding Physical or In Vitro Characterization subcategories. You can display a detail view of the characterization in a table which you can then print or save to a spreadsheet file.
To display a detail view of an existing physical or in vitro characterization, follow these steps. (Asterisks indicate required fields.)
1.
Click the hyperlinked name under the Physical or in vitro Characterization subcategory in the Navigation Tree. The Physical or In Vitro Characterization - [subcategory] page opens.
The following table lists and describes the fields that appear in a details page for each physical or in vitro characterization entry.
If the table is missing information or empty, the fields were not filled in when the characterization was added.

Lists the data derived from the characterization file. The characterization files are numbered in the order they were defined.
