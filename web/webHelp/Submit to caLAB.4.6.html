Nanoparticle Function
Nanoparticle functions describe the kinds of actions nanoparticles can perform, or the purposes for which they are used.
Each function category to which you can add annotations displays a hypertext Add to its right on the Particle Tree. Click the Add link to open a form where you can add the details of function information as corresponding annotation data.
Subcategories of Function include the following:
Therapeutic
Nanoparticles can aid in the delivery of therapeutic agents or antibodies to exact areas of the body. Alternatively, nanoparticles can be activate by lasers, and in giving off heat, destroy tumors or other targeted areas.
Targeting
Nanoparticles can be designed to target tumors, or other
Diagnostic Imaging
Nanoparticles can be directed to exact regions of the body with agents such as radioactive isotopes that display under imaging techniques.
Diagnostic Reporting
When nanoparticles are directed to particular regions in the body, such as tumors, they can be used for diagnostic reporting.
