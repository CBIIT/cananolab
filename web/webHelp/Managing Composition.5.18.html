Defining a Functionalizing Entity
To continue defining Functionalizing Entity fields, follow these steps.
Click [Other] to enter an alternative to selections listed. At that point, your entry is enclosed in [new entry] brackets, indicating that your entry is temporary. Once you click Submit (or Update), your entry is persisted and the brackets disappear
1.
Enter a Chemical Name for the functionalizing entity whose type you just selected.
2.
PubChem Data Source/PubChem ID: Select one of the three PubChem databases: Compound, Substance, and BioAssay. The database is located here: http://pubchem.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/. Enter the PubChem ID which is the PubChem identifier for a compound, a substance or a bioassay.
3.
Amount/Amount Unit: Enter appropriate values and unit for this composing element information. Unit options are:
•
%
•
% mole
•
% vol
•
% wt/vol
4.
Molecular Formula Type: Select from the drop-down list the molecular formula type found in this biopolymer
•
SMARTS
•
SMILES
5.
Molecular Formula: Enter the chemical formula of the functionalizing entity.
6.
Activation Method: Select from the drop-down menu the method used to activate the functionality of the sample. Selection options are:
•
enzymatic cleavage
•
infrared
•
MRI
•
NMR
•
pH
•
ultrasound
•
ultraviolet
7.
Activation Effect: Enter the functional effect of the entity.
8.
Description: Enter any additional composition information that the form does not include.
9.
When you have finished, click Submit to save the data to the sample or click Reset to clear all fields on the form.
After defining the Functionalizing Entity, continue the process described in Adding Functionalizing Entity Composition Annotations.
