Submitting Annotations for Nanoparticles
A nanoparticle is defined as a small, stable particle, whose size is measured in nanometers. These particles are used in biomedical applications in which they serve as drug carriers or imaging agents. Various targeting agents, such as antibodies, drugs, imaging agents, and reporters can be attached to the surface of a nanoparticle.
Note:
In caNanoLab, the term “sample” can refer to either of two entities:
1.
A “Sample” is received from a vendor and accessioned into the laboratory. A sample can be of many types, including a nanoparticle. When a sample is received by the laboratory, the sample is assigned a sample type (can be a nanoparticle), and aliquots are created from the original sample for laboratory testing.
2.
A “Nanoparticle” is a type of sample and is annotated with research and other characterization data. Nanoparticles are annotated in caNanoLab by recording research results (characterizations, result files) that have been obtained in a laboratory.
In online help, the sample initially accessioned into the laboratory (1) is identified as Sample and the sample type (2) is identified as Nanoparticle. This section describes the methods and fields for annotating nanoparticle [sample types].
For detailed instructions on annotating a nanoparticle with characterizations and results, refer to the Related Topics.
