Carbon Nanotube
Carbon nanotubes (CNTs) are fullerene-like nanostructures that consist of graphene cylinders. the ends of the construct are closed with pentagonal-shaped rings.
Each Composition form starts with a summary section that names the particle source. To complete the form, follow these steps:
1.
Fill in each of the following fields on the carbon nanotube characterization form:
Carbon Nanotube Characterization Fields
Definition
Characterization Source
Enter the source from which the characterization data is derived, such as a vendor or a laboratory (NCL).
View Title*
Enter a unique and meaningful name for the characterization. The title you enter will display on the Particle Tree. The asterisk indicates a required field.
Description
Enter any additional characterization information that the form does not include.
Composition Properties
 
Growth Diameter
Enter the measurement of the nanotube diameter, as measured from one side of the tube wall through the center of the nanotube to the opposite point on the circumference.
Chirality
Chirality is the spatial position or orientation of functional groups located within a molecule. The structural identification of stereoisomers is often of interest. Enter information about the chirality in the nanotube and its effect on the orientation of functional groups located on the particle.
Average Length
Enter the average length of the nanotube
Wall Type
Select the appropriate description of the nanotube wall:
•
Single: Wall consists of a single layer of graphitic carbon.
•
Double: Nanotube wall consists of two layers of graphitic carbon.
•
Multiple: Nanotube consists of single-walled nanotubes layered inside each other
Modification Information
 
Number of Modifications
Enter the number of modifications made to the nanotube.
Update Modifications
Note: If you enter any number greater than “0”, click the Update Modifications button at the bottom of the page. This extends the page with the corresponding number of additional fields so you can enter additional information for each unit.
