Metal Particle
A metal particle is a nanoparticle composed of electropositive chemical elements characterized by ductility, malleability, luster, and conductance of heat and electricity. They can replace the hydrogen of an acid and form bases with hydroxyl radicals.
Each Composition form starts with a summary section that names the particle source. To complete the form, follow these steps:
1.
Fill in each of the following fields on the Metal Particle characterization form:
Metal Particle Characterization Fields
Definition
Characterization Source
Enter the source from which the characterization data is derived, such as a vendor or a laboratory (NCL).
View Title*
Enter a unique and meaningful name for the characterization. The title you enter will display on the Particle Tree. The asterisk indicates a required field.
Description
Enter any additional characterization information that the form does not include.
Core Information
 
Chemical Name
The name given to the defined atomic or molecular structure that composes the center of the nanoparticle.
Description
Enter any additional information about the particle other than Chemical name.
Shell and Coating Information
 
Number of Shells
The number of shells this particle possesses.
Number of Coatings
A coating is one or more layers of a substance spread over a surface for protection. Indicate the number of coating(s) making up this particle.
Update Shells and Coatings
Note: If you enter any number greater than “0”, click the Update Shells and Coatings button at the bottom of the page. This extends the page with the corresponding number of additional fields so you can enter information for each unit.
