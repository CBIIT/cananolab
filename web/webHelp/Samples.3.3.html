Description of a Sample
A sample is created and assigned an ID when it arrives at the laboratory. A sample coming into a nanotech laboratory is a nanoparticle. Multiple lots for the same sample can be received. Because of that, a lot id is appended to the sample ID. NCL-22-1 (sample ID “22” + lot ID “1”). When performing an experiment, multiple aliquots for each sample/lot are created and experimental data is submitted for these aliquots in the workflow. When the experiment is complete and the researcher wants to publish the outcome of the experiment, the final results file associated with the sample + lot are submitted/selected into the system. The final results apply to all aliquots associated with the sample and lot. Annotations are recorded at the nanoparticle sample level.
 
Note: In caNanoLab, the term “sample” can refer to either of two entities:
1.
A “Sample” is received from a vendor and accessioned into the laboratory. A sample can be of many types, including a nanoparticle. When a sample is received by the laboratory, the sample is assigned a sample type (can be a nanoparticle), and aliquots are created from the original sample for laboratory testing.
2.
A “Nanoparticle” is a type of sample and is annotated with research and other characterization data. Nanoparticles are annotated in caNanoLab by recording research results (characterizations, result files) that have been obtained in a laboratory.
In online help, the sample initially accessioned into the laboratory (1) is identified as Sample and the sample type (2) is identified as Nanoparticle. This section describes the methods and fields for annotating nanoparticle [sample types].
