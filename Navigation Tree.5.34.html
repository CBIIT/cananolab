In Vitro Characterization Category
Nanoparticle characterization, describing distinctive characteristics or essential features of the nanoparticle determined through analytical methods, is necessary to record information associated with nanoparticle synthesis and properties. Nanoparticles can be characterized in caNanoLab by physical characteristics and by in vitro characteristics. In Vitro Characterization is the determination of the effect of nanoparticles on living cells in an artificial laboratory environment outside of the living organism.
When you click the Navigation Tree InVitro Characterization button, the subcategories expand under the heading (Click the button again to collapse the subcategories). You can annotate nanoparticles with laboratory results obtained under in vitro conditions. If annotation information is filled out for the nanoparticle, the annotations are listed under the subcategories. You may have the option to add or delete function details with the Add and Delete button.
Most of the fields on the forms that open for these in vitro characterization categories are identical. They differ only when you click Number of Histogram/Bar Charts, enter the number and click Update. At that point, the page extends, and for different characterizations, the value names are different, as noted in the tables described in the topics.
For information specific to each Toxicity subcategory, see Toxicity Subcategory.
